AI SaaS  ·  Data Analytics

Eliminating the Data Analyst Bottleneck with AI-powered Self-Serve Analytics

Fortune 500 companies running large data platforms faced a critical bottleneck: a handful of analysts knew the data well, but couldn't keep pace with executive demand for reports and insights. Wait times stretched to weeks, delaying business decisions. The challenge was building an AI system that could generate accurate, trusted SQL and visualizations autonomously — without replacing the human judgment that mattered.

The Problem

  • Analyst capacity was the single bottleneck for insight generation across multiple enterprise customers
  • Executives waited weeks for reports needed for time-sensitive decisions
  • Existing BI tools required significant analyst mediation to interpret business intent correctly
  • No mechanism to encode and reuse institutional data knowledge at scale

What We Built

  • AI workflow that maps a natural language question to relevant tables using semantic retrieval and knowledge graph traversal
  • Context enrichment layer — business glossary, data dictionary, and golden SQLs embedded via knowledge graph
  • LLM-powered SQL generation with multi-layer validation: statistical checks, semantic guards, and destructive-operation guardrails
  • Human-in-the-loop feedback loop before execution, allowing users to refine and approve SQL
  • Code-generated visualizations passed back to an LLM to produce plain-language business insights
  • Full SaaS layer: microservices, user auth, payment gateway, feature flagging, user memory, and cost-optimized inference across two teams

My Role

  • Designed the complete AI workflow architecture from scratch — end-to-end
  • Led technology stack decisions: vector store selection, eval framework, metadata strategy, inference optimization
  • Defined information hierarchy and context minimization strategy to reduce LLM cost without accuracy loss
  • Guided two engineering teams on inference cost optimization
  • Presented architecture and progress directly to executive stakeholders in recurring design reviews
